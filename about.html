--- layout: null section-type: about title: About --- ## About

I am a Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing Researcher affiliated with Masakhane, an open source and distributed research effort for NLP for African languages. I'm also collaborating with Doug Downey and the Semantic Scholar team at AI2 on scientific article summary generation.

Previously, I worked on conditional language generation, form understanding, and optical character recognition at Scale AI and fake speech detection and synthesis at AI Foundation. I completed my MS in Computer Science at the Courant Institute at NYU in the CILVR group focusing on deep learning applied to natural language processing and advised by Kyunghyun Cho and Sam Bowman. Before this I completed my B.A. in Statistics and M.S. in Computer Science focusing on machine learning and natural language processing at Northwestern University. My advisor was Doug Downey.

Broadly, my research interests are:

  • Natural Language Processing
  • Causality
  • ML for Social Good
  • Conditional Language Modeling
  • Sequence Generation
  • Representation Learning
